We can generalize this Euclidean metric from \(\R^2\) to any dimensional real space. Let \(n\) be a positive integer and let \(x = (x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)\) and \(y = (y_1, y_2, \ldots, y_n)\) be in \(\R^n\text{.}\) We define \(d_E : \R^n \times \R^n \to \R\) by
\begin{equation*} d_E(x,y) = \sqrt{(x_1-y_1)^2 + (x_2-y_2)^2 + \cdots (x_n-y_n)^2} = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^n (x_i-y_i)^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
