Let \(X\) be a metric space and \(O\) a subset of \(X\text{.}\) To prove this biconditional statement we first assume that \(O\) is an open set and demonstrate that \(O\) is a union of open balls. Let \(a \in O\text{.}\) Since \(O\) is open, there exists \(\epsilon_a \gt 0\) so that \(B(a, \epsilon_a) \subseteq O\text{.}\) We will show that
\begin{equation*} O = \bigcup_{a \in O} B(a, \epsilon_a)\text{.} \end{equation*}
