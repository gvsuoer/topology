Definition 10.3.

Let \(X\) be a metric space, and let \(A\) be a subset of \(X\text{.}\)
  1. An accumulation point or limit point of \(A\) is a point \(x \in X\) such that every neighborhood of \(x\) contains a point in \(A\) different from \(x\text{.}\)
  2. An isolated point of \(A\) is a point \(a \in A\) such that there exists a neighborhood \(N\) of \(a\) in \(X\) with \(N \cap A = \{a\}\text{.}\)
