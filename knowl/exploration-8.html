Preview Activity 9.1.

(a)

Explain why the sequence \(\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\) converges to 0 in \(\R\) using the Euclidean metric \(d_E\text{,}\) where
\begin{equation*} d_E(a,y) = | x-y |\text{.} \end{equation*}

(b)

Consider the sequence \((a_n) = \left(\left(\frac{1}{n}, \frac{1}{n+1} \right)\right)\) in \((\R^2, d_T)\text{,}\) where \(d_T\) is the taxicab metric
\begin{equation*} d_T((x_1, x_2), (y_1, y_1)) = | x_1-y_1| + | x_2-y_2 |\text{.} \end{equation*}
Does the sequence \((a_n)\) converge? If so, find its limit and prove that your candidate is the limit. If not, explain why.

(c)

Let \((b_n) = \left((2n, n^2)\right)\) in the metric space \((\R^2, d)\text{,}\) where \(d\) is the discrete metric defined by
\begin{equation*} d(x,y) = \begin{cases}0 \amp \text{ if } x=y \\ 1 \amp \text{ if } x \neq y. \end{cases} \end{equation*}
Does the sequence \((b_n)\) converge? If so, find its limit and prove that your candidate is the limit. If not, explain why.
