Many other interesting identification spaces can be made. For example, let \(X = I \times I\) and define \(\sim\) by \((x, y) \sim (x,y)\) if \(0 \lt x \lt 1\) and \(0 \lt y \lt 1\text{,}\) \((0, y) \sim (1, y)\) for \(0 \lt y \lt 1\text{,}\) \((x,0) \sim (1-x,1)\) for \(0 \lt x \lt 1\text{.}\) This identification is illustrated in Figure 16.6. The resulting identification space \(X/\ssim\) is a Klein bottle. A nice illustration of this can be seen at maths.org 8 .
