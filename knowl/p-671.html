We begin by working with continuous functions from \(\R\) to \(\R\text{.}\) Our goal is to make more rigorous our informal definition of continuity at a point. To do so will require us to formally defining what we mean by
  • making the values of \(f(x)\) “as close to \(f(a)\) as we want”, and
  • choosing \(x\) “as close to \(a\) as we need”.
