Activity 11.3.

In this activity we verify some of the properties that make \(d\) as defined in (11.1) a metric. Let \(x=(x_1,x_2)\) and \(y=(y_1,y_2)\) be in \(X_1 \times X_2\)

(a)

Explain why \(d(x,y)\) is greater than or equal to \(0\text{.}\)

(b)

Explain why \(d(x,y) = d(y,x)\text{.}\)

(c)

Explain why \(d(x,y) = 0\) if and only if \(x = y\text{.}\)
