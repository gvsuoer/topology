Explain why \(S\) contains an integer \(m\) such that if \(q \in \Z\) with \(q \lt m\text{,}\) then \(q \leq nx\text{.}\) It may be helpful to use the Well-Ordering Principle that states Every subset of the integers that is bounded below contains its infimum. (The Well-Ordering Principle is one of many axioms that are equivalent to the Principle of Mathematical Induction. These principles are taken as axioms and are assumed to be true.)
