Suppose \(X\) is a topological space and \(Y\) is a set, and let \(p: X \to Y\) be a surjection. We can define a relation \(\sim_p\) on \(X\) by \(x \sim_p y\) if and only if \(p(x) = p(y)\text{.}\) It is straightforward to show that \(\sim_p\) is an equivalence relation, the details are left for ExerciseÂ 1. From this we can see that our two approaches to defining the quotient topology and quotient spaces are really the same.
