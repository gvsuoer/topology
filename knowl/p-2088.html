For \(i \in \Z^+\text{,}\) let \(Q_i = [a_{i,1}, b_{i,1}] \times [a_{i,2}, b_{i,2}] \times \cdots [a_{i,n}, b_{i,n}]\text{.}\) That is, think of the point \((a_{i,1}, a_{i,2}, \ldots, a_{i,n})\) as a lower corner of the cube and the point \((b_{i,1}, b_{i,2}, \ldots, b_{i,n})\) as an upper corner of the \(n\)-cube \(Q_i\) (a labeling for \(n=2\) and \(i\) from 1 to 3 is shown at right in FigureÂ 17.11). Let \(q = (\sup\{a_{i,1}\}, \sup\{a_{i,2}\}, \ldots, \sup\{a_{i,n}\})\text{.}\) We will show that \(q \in \bigcap_{k=1}^{\infty} Q_k\text{.}\) Fix \(r \in \Z^+\text{.}\) We need to demonstrate that
\begin{equation*} q \in Q_r = \{(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n) \mid a_{r,s} \leq x_s \leq b_{r,s} \text{ for each } 1 \leq s \leq n\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
