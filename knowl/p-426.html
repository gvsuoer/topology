The taxicab metric can be extended to \(\R^n\) for any \(n \geq 1\) as follows. If \(x = (x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)\) and \(y = (y_1, y_2, \ldots, y_n)\) are in \(\R^n\text{,}\) then the taxicab distance \(d_T(x,y)\) from \(x\) to \(y\) is defined as
\begin{equation*} d_T(x,y) = |x_1-y_1| + |x_2-y_2| + \cdots + |x_n-y_n| = \sum_{i=1}^n |x_i-y_i|\text{.} \end{equation*}
