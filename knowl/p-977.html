Let \(N \gt \frac{1}{\epsilon} -1\) (so that \(N\) depends on \(\epsilon\)). Then for \(n \geq N\) we have
\begin{align*} n \amp \gt N \gt \frac{1}{\epsilon} -1\\ 1+n \amp \gt \frac{1}{\epsilon}\\ |-1| \left| \frac{1}{1+n} \right| \amp \lt \epsilon\\ \left| \frac{-1}{1+n} \right| \amp \lt \epsilon\\ \left| \frac{n}{1+n} - 1 \right| \amp \lt \epsilon\\ \left| \frac{n}{1+n} - \frac{1+n}{1+n} \right| \amp \lt \epsilon\text{.} \end{align*}
