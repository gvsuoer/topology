By the way we chose \(\epsilon_a\text{,}\) \(B(a, \epsilon_a) \subseteq O\) for every \(a \in O\text{.}\) So \(\bigcup_{a \in O} B(a, \epsilon_a) \subseteq O\text{.}\) For the reverse containment, let \(x \in O\text{.}\) Then \(x \in B(x, \epsilon_x)\) and so \(x \in \bigcup_{a \in O} B(a, \epsilon_a)\text{.}\) Thus, \(O \subseteq \bigcup_{a \in O} B(a, \epsilon_a)\text{.}\) We conclude that \(O\) is a union of open balls if \(O\) is an open set.
