Definition 16.1.

Let \((X,\tau_X)\) be a topological space, let \(Y\) be a set, and let \(p: X \to Y\) be a surjection.
  1. The quotient topology on \(Y\) is the set
    \begin{equation*} \{U \subseteq Y \mid p^{-1}(U) \in \tau_X\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Any function \(p: X \to Y\) is a quotient map if \(p\) is surjective and for \(U \subseteq Y\text{,}\) \(U\) is open in \(Y\) if and only if \(p^{-1}(U)\) is open in \(X\text{.}\)
  3. If \(p: X \to Y\) is a quotient map, then the space \(Y\) is the quotient space of \(X\) determined by \(p\text{.}\)
