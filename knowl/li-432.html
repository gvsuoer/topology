Item.

In what follows we demonstrate that \(m^2 = 2\text{,}\) which makes \(m = \sqrt{2}\text{.}\) We consider the cases \(m^2 \lt 2\) and \(m^2 > 2\text{.}\)
  1. Suppose \(m^2 \lt 2\text{.}\) Show that there is a positive integer \(n\) such that
    \begin{equation*} \left(m+\frac{1}{n}\right)^2~\lt ~2\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Explain why this also cannot happen.
  2. Suppose \(m^2 > 2\text{.}\) Show that there is a positive integer \(n\) such that
    \begin{equation*} \left(m-\frac{1}{n}\right)^2~>~2\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Explain why this also cannot happen.
