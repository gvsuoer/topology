Item.

To create a contradiction, we will construct a sequence \((a_n)\) that converges to \(a\) while \((f(a_n))\) does not converge to \(f(a)\text{.}\)
  1. Explain why we can find a positive integer \(K\) such that \(\frac{1}{K} \lt \epsilon\text{.}\)
  2. If \(k > K\text{,}\) explain why there is an element \(a_k \in B\left(a, \frac{1}{k}\right)\) so that \(d_Y(f(a_k), f(a)) \geq \epsilon\text{.}\)
  3. For \(k \leq K\text{,}\) let \(a_k\) be any element in \(B\left(a, \frac{1}{k}\right)\text{.}\) Explain why \(a\) is a limit of \((a_n)\text{.}\)
  4. Explain why \(f(a)\) is not a limit of the sequence \((f(a_n))\text{.}\) What conclusion can we draw, and why?
