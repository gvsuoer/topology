Activity 20.5.3.

Let \(X\) be a locally path connected topological space. In this activity we will prove that the components and path components of \(X\) are the same.
  • Let \(x \in X\text{,}\) and let \(C\) be the component of \(X\) containing \(x\) and \(P\) be the path component of \(X\) containing \(x\text{.}\) Show that \(P \subseteq C\text{.}\)
  • To complete the proof that \(P = C\text{,}\) proceed by contradiction and assume that \(C \neq P\text{.}\) Let \(Q\) be the union of all path components of \(X\) that are different from \(P\) and that intersect \(C\text{.}\) Each such path component is connected, and is therefore a subset of \(C\text{.}\) So \(C = P \cup Q\text{.}\) Explain why \(P\) and \(Q\) form a separation of \(C\text{.}\) Hint.
