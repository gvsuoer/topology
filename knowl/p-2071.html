The metric space \((\R^n,d_E)\) is not compact since the open cover \(\{B(0, n)\}_{n \in \Z^+}\) has no finite sub-cover. Since we have already shown that \((\R,d_E)\) is homeomorphic to the topological subspaces \((a,b)\text{,}\) \((-\infty, b)\text{,}\) and \((a,\infty)\) for any \(a, b \in \R\text{,}\) we conclude that no open intervals are compact. Similarly, no half-closed intervals are compact. In fact, we will demonstrate in this section that the compact subsets of \((\R^n, d_E)\) are exactly the subsets that are closed and bounded. The first step is contained in the next activity.
