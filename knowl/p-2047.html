There are two perspectives by which we can look at compactness. If \((X,\tau_X)\) is a topological space and \(A\) is a subset of \(X\text{,}\) then Definition 17.1 tells us what it means for \(A\) to be compact as a subset of \(X\text{.}\) From this perspective, we use open sets in \(X\) to make open covers of \(A\text{.}\) We can also consider \(A\) as a subspace of \(X\) using the subspace topology \(\tau_A\text{.}\) From this perspective we can examine the compactness of \(A\) using relatively open sets for open covers. Exercise 14 tells us that these two perspectives are equivalent, so we will use whatever perspective is appropriate for a given situation.
