Case 2: \(a \leq 0\) and \(b \leq 0\)
In this case \(a = -a'\) and \(b = -b'\) where \(a'\) and \(b'\) are nonnegative. It follows from Case 1 that
\begin{align*} | a+b | \amp = | -(a'+b') | = | a'+b' | = a'+b' = | a' | + | b' |\\ \amp = | -a' | + | -b' | = | a | + | b |\text{.} \end{align*}
