Item 6.

Let \(f\) and \(g\) be continuous functions from \((\R, d_E)\) to \((\R,d_E)\text{.}\) In this exercise we will prove that \(fg\) is a continuous function from \(\R\) to \(\R\text{.}\) Let \(a\) be in \(\R\text{,}\) and follow the steps below to show that \(fg\) is continuous at \(x=a\text{.}\) Let \(\epsilon\) be a positive number.
  • We will first want to express \(f(x)g(x) - f(a)g(a)\) in a more useful way. Use the fact that \(f(x) = f(a) + (f(x)-f(a))\) and \(g(x) = g(a) + (g(x)-g(a))\) to show that
    \begin{equation*} f(x)g(x)-f(a)g(a) = f(a)(g(x)-g(a)) + g(a)(f(x)-f(a)) + (f(x)-f(a))(g(x)-g(a))\text{.} \end{equation*}
  • Explain why there exist positive numbers \(\delta_1\text{,}\) \(\delta_2\text{,}\) \(\delta_3\text{,}\) and \(\delta_4\) such that
    \begin{align*} |f(x)-f(a)| \lt \sqrt{\frac{\epsilon}{3}} \amp \text{ when } |x-a| \lt \delta_1\\ |g(x)-g(a)| \lt \sqrt{\frac{\epsilon}{3}} \amp \text{ when } |x-a| \lt \delta_2\\ |f(x)-f(a)| \lt \frac{\epsilon}{3(1+|g(a)|)} \amp \text{ when } |x-a| \lt \delta_3\\ |g(x)-g(a)| \lt \frac{\epsilon}{3(1+|f(a)|)} \amp \text{ when } |x-a| \lt \delta_4\text{.} \end{align*}
  • Use the results of (a) and (b) to show that \(fg\) is continuous at \(x=a\text{.}\) (Hint: \(1+|f(a)| > |f(a)|\text{.}\))
