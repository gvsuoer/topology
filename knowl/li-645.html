Item 2.

Consider the sequence \((a_n) = \left(\left(\frac{1}{n}, \frac{1}{n+1} \right)\right)\) in \((\R^2, d_T)\text{,}\) where \(d_T\) is the taxicab metric
\begin{equation*} d_T((x_1, x_2), (y_1, y_1)) = | x_1-y_1| + | x_2-y_2 |\text{.} \end{equation*}
Does the sequence \((a_n)\) converge? If so, find its limit and prove that your candidate is the limit. If not, explain why.
