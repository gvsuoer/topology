Task 6.1.c.

(i)
What is the negation of the definition of continuity at a point? In other words, what do we need to do to show that a function \(f: \R \to \R\) is not continuous at a point \(x=a\text{?}\)
(ii)

\begin{equation*} f(x) = \begin{cases}-1 \amp \text{ if } x \lt 1 \\ 1 \amp \text{ if } x \geq 1 \end{cases} \end{equation*}
is not continuous at \(x=1\text{.}\)
