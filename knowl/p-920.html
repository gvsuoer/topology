As an example of CorollaryÂ 8.6, we prove that the square function from \(\R\) to \(\R\) is a continuous function. Let \(X = \R\) with the Euclidean metric \(d_E\text{,}\) and let \(f: X \to X\) be defined by \(f(x) = x^2\text{.}\) We will show that \(f\) is a continuous function by verifying that \(f^{-1}(B)\) is open in \(X\) for every open ball \(B\) in \(X\text{.}\) Let \(B = B(b,\beta) = (b-\beta, b+\beta)\) be an open ball in \(X\text{.}\) Let \(B' = B(b,\beta) \cap (\R^+ \cup \{0\})\text{.}\) We consider cases.
  • Suppose that \(B' = \emptyset\text{.}\) Then \(f^{-1}(B) = \emptyset\) and \(f^{-1}(B)\) is open in \(X\text{.}\)
  • Suppose that \(B' = [0, b+\beta)\text{.}\) Then \(f^{-1}(B) = (-\sqrt{b+\beta}, \sqrt{b+\beta})\) and \(f^{-1}(B)\) is open in \(X\text{.}\)
  • The final case is \(B' = (b-\beta, b+\beta)\text{.}\) Then
    \begin{equation*} f^{-1}(B) = (-\sqrt{b+\beta}, -\sqrt{b-\beta}) \cup (\sqrt{b-\beta}, \sqrt{b+\beta}) \end{equation*}
    and \(f^{-1}(B)\) is open in \(X\text{.}\)
