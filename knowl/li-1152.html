Item 5.

Let \((X, \tau_X)\text{,}\) \((Y, \tau_Y)\text{,}\) and \((Z, \tau_Z)\) be topological spaces.
  • Let \(f: X \to Y\) and \(g : Y \to Z\) be continuous functions. Prove that \(g \circ f : X \to Z\) is a continuous function. Hint.
  • Let \(f: (X, \tau_X) \to (Y, \tau_Y)\) be a homeomorphism. Let \(h\) be a function from \((Y, \tau_Y)\) to \((Z, \tau_Z)\) and let \(k\) be a function from \((Z, \tau_Z)\) to \((X, \tau_X)\) .
    1. Prove that \(h\) is continuous if and only if \(h \circ f\) is continuous.
    2. Prove that \(k\) is continuous if and only if \(f \circ k\) is continuous.
