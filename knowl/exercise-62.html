Exercise 8.

Let \(f(x) = 2x^2+1\) map from \(\R\) to \(\R\text{,}\) with both the domain and codomain having the Euclidean metric.

(a)

Let \(\epsilon = \frac{1}{4}\text{.}\) Find a value of \(\delta\) such that \(|x-1| \lt \delta\) implies that \(|f(x)-f(a)| \lt \epsilon\text{.}\) You might use the applet at to confirm your value of \(\delta\text{.}\)

(b)

Prove that \(f\) is continuous at \(x=1\text{.}\)
