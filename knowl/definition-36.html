Definition 11.4.

Let \((X_i, d_i)\) be metric spaces for \(i\) from \(1\) to some positive integer \(n\text{.}\) The product metric space \((X,d)\) is the Cartesian product
\begin{equation*} X = X_1 \times X_2 \times \cdots \times X_n = \prod_{i=1}^n X_i \end{equation*}
with metric \(d\) defined by
\begin{equation*} d(x,y) = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^n d_i(x_i,y_i)^2} \end{equation*}
when \(x = (x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_n)\) and \(y = (y_1, y_2, \ldots, y_n)\) are in \(X\text{.}\)
