One might expect that the interior of a set is an open set. This is true, but we can say even more. As TheoremÂ 8.10 will show, if \(A\) is a subset of a metric space \(X\text{,}\) not only is \(\Int(A)\) an open set, but every open set that is contained in \(A\) is a subset of \(\Int(A)\text{.}\) So \(\Int(A)\) is the largest, in the sense of containment, open subset of \(X\) that contains \(A\text{.}\)
