The word “compact” might bring to mind a notion of smallness, but we need to be careful with the term. We might think that the interval \((0, 0.5)\) is small, but \((0, 0.5)\) is homeomorphic to \(\R\text{,}\) which is not small. Similarly, we might think that the interval \([-10000, 10000]\) is large, but this interval is homeomorphic to the “small” interval \([-0.00001, 0.000001]\text{.}\) As a result, the concept of compactness does not correspond to size, but rather structure, in a way. We will expand on this idea in this section.
