The space \(X\) is regular if for each closed set \(C\) of \(X\) and each point \(x \in X \setminus C\text{,}\) there exists disjoint open sets \(U\) and \(V\) in \(X\) such that \(C \subseteq U\) and \(x \in V\text{.}\) The space \(X\) is a T\(_3\)-space or a regular Hausdorff space if \(X\) is a regular \(T_1\) space.
