A reasonable question to ask is if a limit of a sequence is unique. We will answer that question in this activity. Let \((X,d)\) be a metric space and \((a_n)\) a sequence in \(X\text{.}\) Assume the sequence \((a_n)\) has a limit in \(X\text{.}\) To show that a limit of the sequence \((a_n)\) is unique, we need to show that if \(\lim a_n = a\) and \(\lim a_n = a'\) for some \(a, a' \in X\text{,}\) then \(a=a'\text{.}\)
