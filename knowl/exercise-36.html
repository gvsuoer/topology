Exercise 9.

Let \((X, d_X)\) and \((Y, d_Y)\) be metric spaces. We can make the Cartesian product \(X \times Y\) into a metric space by defining a metric \(d'\) on \(X \times Y\) as follows. If \((x_1, y_2)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\) are in \(X \times Y\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} d'((x_1,y_1), (x_2, y_2)) = \max\{d_X(x_1,x_2), d_Y(y_1,y_2)\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
You may assume without proof that \(d'\) is a metric on \(X \times Y\text{.}\)

(a)

Let \((X, d_X) = (\R^2, d_M)\) and \((Y, d_Y) = (\R^2, d_T)\text{.}\) Let \(u = ((1,2), (1,-1))\) and \(v = ((0,5),(2,-2))\text{.}\) What is
\begin{equation*} d'(u,v)? \end{equation*}
Recall that
\begin{equation*} d_M((x_1,x_2),(y_1,y_2)) = \max\{ | x_1-y_1 |, | x_2-y_2 |\} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} d_T((x_1,x_2),(y_1,y_2)) = | x_1-y_1 | + | x_2-y_2 |\text{.} \end{equation*}

(b)

Let \((X, d_X) = (\R, d_E)\) and \((Y, d_Y) = (\R, d)\text{,}\) where \(d\) is the discrete metric. Let
\begin{equation*} A=\{(x,y) \in \R \times \R \mid -1\leq x \leq 1 \text{ and } -1 \leq y \leq 1\} \end{equation*}
in \(X \times Y\text{.}\) Let \(a = (0,1)\) in \(X \times Y\text{.}\) Describe, geometrically, what the open ball \(B(a,1)\) looks like in the product space \(X \times Y\text{.}\) Draw a picture of this open ball.
