Corollary 18.4.

Let \((X, \tau_X)\) and \((Y, \tau_Y)\) be homeomorphic topological spaces. Then \(X\) is connected if and only if \(Y\) is connected.

Proof.

Let \((X, \tau_X)\) and \((Y, \tau_Y)\) be topological spaces and let \(f: X \to Y\) be a homeomorphism. Assume that \(X\) is connected. Since \(f\) is continuous, TheoremÂ 18.3 shows that \(f(X) = Y\) is connected. The reverse implication follows from the fact that \(f^{-1}\) is a homeomorphism.
